Humanities

Economics as History, Not Nature

Three critical histories that take what feels most natural about money and markets and show, from the record, that it was made, finishing with the deeper argument about what money actually is.

  1. 1
    The Myth of Barter
    Start concrete: where money actually came from, and why the barter origin story is a myth.
  2. 2
    How Capitalism Came to Seem Natural
    Then the whole system: how a recent, built economic order came to feel like human nature.
  3. 3
    What Money Actually Is
    Finish with rigor: the sequel to The Myth of Barter, adjudicating commodity versus credit theory and refusing the verdict the evidence will not support.
