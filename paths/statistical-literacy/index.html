Statistical Literacy

Go from no statistics at all to reading a study, a poll, or a chart without being fooled.

Most of us meet statistics as a wall of jargon and take the claims on trust. This path takes you from zero to genuinely statistically literate — able to look at a headline number, a poll, or a graph and ask the right question. It begins with the Essentials primer that gives you the vocabulary, then applies it to the two situations you meet most: judging a study, and reading a relationship between two things.

By the end you can
  • Explain what a sample can and can't tell you about a population.
  • Spot the common misreadings of p-values and confidence intervals.
  • Tell a real relationship from a coincidence in a chart.
