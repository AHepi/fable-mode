Pure Mathematics, From the Ground Up

Build the two pillars of modern pure maths — algebraic structure and topological space — as ideas, not just symbols.

Two courses that together open the door to modern pure mathematics. Abstract algebra teaches you to reason about structure — what stays true when you only know the rules of an operation. Topology teaches you to reason about space — nearness and continuity stripped of distance. Take them in either order; together they are the vocabulary the rest of advanced maths is written in.

By the end you can
  • Reason fluently about abstract structures and their maps.
  • See why topology generalises geometry, and what 'continuous' really means.
