Turn a square coaster a quarter turn and it looks untouched. Add five hours to nine o'clock and you
land on two. Flip a triangle and spin it, or spin it and flip it, and you end up somewhere different
each time. These look like three unrelated curiosities. They are the same idea in three disguises —
and that idea, *structure*, is what this course is about.

Abstract algebra is the study of operations and the rules they obey, set free from the particular
things being operated on. Its central character is the **group**: a set with one operation and four
simple rules. From that small seed grows a way of seeing symmetry inside arithmetic, geometry, and
the equations you already know.

We start from scratch — no proofs assumed, nothing beyond high-school algebra and geometry required.
You'll warm up on clock arithmetic, distill the rules that matter, and assemble them into the
definition of a group. Then you'll hunt for groups in the wild, learn to find the smaller groups
hiding inside them, discover when two groups are secretly identical, and finish by widening the lens
to rings and fields — the structures that organize all of modern algebra. Each module ends with
exercises whose full worked solutions are a click away, and pauses along the way to let you try an
idea from memory before it shows you the answer.
