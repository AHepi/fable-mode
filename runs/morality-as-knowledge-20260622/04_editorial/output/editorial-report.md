## A. Attribution ledger (the top risk) — HOLDS

Verified across all ten modules against canon §1b.

- **Hume / Moore / Mackie / Ayer distinct, never fused.** Module 03 keeps the "three
  different keys" device clean: Hume = a broken *inference* (is/ought, no valid deduction);
  Moore = a failed *definition* (naturalistic fallacy / open-question, about meaning not
  inference); Mackie = error theory + queerness (claims aim at fact, all false). The
  Mackie/Ayer contrast is drawn explicitly and correctly in 03 (Ayer: states *nothing*,
  no truth-value; Mackie: states something *false*) and again in the 03 retrieval check.
  Module 02 keeps relativism (local truth) and Ayer's emotivism (no truth-value, driven by
  a theory of meaning) genuinely separate. No blending found.
- **Popper / Deutsch seam — clean.** Popper owns fallibilism, anti-justificationism, the
  induction dissolution, conjecture-and-criticism (04), and the open society (08). Deutsch
  owns the no-special-problem move (05), hard-to-vary + reach (06), the moral application
  and the aesthetics extension (06, 09), and the optimism + knowledge-to-good link (08).
  Module 05's hinge attributes the *combining* move to Deutsch building on Popper, and the
  05 retrieval check states the split ("Popper supplied the induction result; Deutsch made
  the moral application"). Module 08 splits the open society (Popper) from the optimism and
  "all evils" (Deutsch) in both prose and its retrieval check. No sentence credits Popper
  with "hard to vary" or the moral move, or Deutsch with induction/the open society.
- **Bold readings attributed as theirs.** Deutsch's "all evils are due to insufficient
  knowledge" appears once (08), attributed to Deutsch and flagged contestable, steelmanned
  before being doubted. Street's and Joyce's evolutionary debunking arguments are dated and
  sourced in 10 ("A Darwinian Dilemma," 2006; *The Evolution of Morality*, 2006) and
  presented as a live objection, not the course's view. Quasi-realism is attributed to
  Simon Blackburn in 10.

## B. No overclaim; the close stays open — HOLDS

No module says or implies the argument *proves* moral realism. Module 05 marks the result
as parity, not realism ("a defense, not a proof"); 06 now ends on "whether it carries all
the way to mind-independent moral facts is a further question, and one the course leaves
open until the end"; 07 bounds its claim explicitly; 09 states it proves nothing about
morality (a parallel, not a link). Module 10 crowns no winner: "The course crowns no winner
between realism and anti-realism, because in the present state of the argument there is no
winner to crown," and the takeaway is the method (hold any morality as a conjecture), not a
verdict. No overreaching sentence found.
