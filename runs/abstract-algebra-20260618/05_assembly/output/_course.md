Turn a square coaster a quarter turn and it looks untouched. Add five hours to nine o'clock and you
land on two. Flip a triangle and spin it, or spin it and flip it, and you end up somewhere different
each time. These look like three unrelated curiosities. They are the same idea in three disguises —
and that idea, *structure*, is what this course is about.

Group theory is the mathematics of structure: of operations and the rules they obey, of symmetry made
exact. You will build it from the ground up — clock arithmetic, the four rules that make a *group*,
the symmetries of a triangle, subgroups and generators, and the one theorem (Lagrange's) where
counting alone forces the truth — and end at rings and fields, the doorway to the rest of algebra.
Every idea is shown, not just stated: operation tables you can read, symmetries you can see, and one
proof walked slowly enough to follow without ever having met a proof before. No calculus, no prior
proof experience — just high-school algebra and a willingness to look closely.
