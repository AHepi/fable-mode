  Artemision of Ephesus recovered early electrum from the temple's foundation deposits, among

**Flagged specifics I checked and stand behind (kept general, no invention):**
- **06 Artemision** (Ephesus, early-20th-c. excavation, foundation-deposit electrum,
  ~late 7th/early 6th c. BCE): solid, kept general.
- **08 Vedic three debts** (ṛṇatraya — to gods/sacrifice, sages/study, ancestors/offspring;

- **10 Keynes "Babylonian madness"**: Keynes did work for years on ancient near-eastern

  Writings* as "Keynes and Ancient Currencies." The "Babylonian madness" phrasing (from a

  characterization ("he wrote… that the old currency had become an uncontrollable madness…
  His Babylonian madness, he called it"). The surrounding facts (six years' work ~1920–26,
  reorganizing the *Treatise* around money of account, the unpublished fragments) are solid.
  If a reviewer cannot confirm the exact "Babylonian madness" wording, recommend softening
  to the solid core. Left as authored per "keep general where solid"; surfaced here for the
  gate.
- **09 BoE 2014 / McLeay-Radia-Thomas**: the paper *Money creation in the modern economy*

  the author and remain omitted (no fabrication risk). Fine.
- **03 cowrie route** (Maldives → West Africa via European carriers, Bight of Benin, slave
  trade): broad fact established; specifics kept rough. Stands.
- **07 Solon seisachtheia** (~594 BCE; Aristotle's *Constitution of the Athenians*,
