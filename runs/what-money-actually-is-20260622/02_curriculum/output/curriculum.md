## The arc

Theory-and-evidence interleaved, not history-first (the reader has the history from the intro course; what they lack is the two theories at full strength and the adjudication). The front half arms both theories; **module 5 (the silver problem) is the hinge** where they collide on one body of evidence; the back half widens to coinage, debt, the over-reach, and modern money, then closes on what is settled, what is open, and how to weigh the next claim. Schumpeter's monetary-vs-real distinction (module 1) is the sorting tool used throughout.

## Module specs

### 01 — The Question We Left Open
- **slug:** `01-the-question-we-left-open`
- **summary:** The intro course killed the barter sequence but left the deeper question open, and a frame from Schumpeter sorts the whole quarrel.
- **objectives:**
  - Restate the open question precisely: is money fundamentally a commodity or a unit of account and measure of debt.
  - Distinguish Schumpeter's "monetary analysis" from "real analysis" and assign the commodity and credit theories to the right side.
  - Explain the course's contract: steelman both sides, crown no winner where the field has not.
- **prereqs:** none (assumes the intro course's vocabulary)
- **est. minutes:** 26
- **notes:** Recall the intro verdict in *one paragraph*; do not re-teach (recall the hinge vocabulary in a clause each). Introduce **Schumpeter's monetary-vs-real-analysis** distinction (his) as the sorting backbone and spiral it back later. Set the steelman-first, no-winner contract that governs the whole course. Owns: the open question, Schumpeter's frame. Closing story: Schumpeter writing *History of Economic Analysis* in his last years (pub. posthumously 1954). **[verify the Elizabeth Boody editing detail.]**

### 02 — The Commodity Theory at Full Strength
- **slug:** `02-the-commodity-theory`
- **summary:** Money as the most saleable commodity the market converges on, grounded by Mises's regression theorem, with the real case for why metal won.
- **objectives:**
  - Explain Menger's account of money as the spontaneous emergence of the most saleable commodity.
  - Explain Mises's regression theorem and how it grounds money's value in a former commodity value, distinct from Menger's emergence story.
  - Assess the concrete "why metal" case (durable, divisible, portable, recognizable, scarce).
- **prereqs:** 01
- **est. minutes:** 30
- **notes:** Pure steelman; nothing is criticized yet (this module establishes the steelman-first discipline for the course). **Keep Menger and Mises distinct** (emergence narrative vs. the value-theoretic regression theorem; gloss the theorem plainly, do not formalize). Don't let the dead barter *sequence* drag down the live commodity *theory of money's nature*. Owns: saleability/Menger, the regression theorem/Mises, "why metal." Closing story: assaying and the touchstone, merchants testing and weighing bullion across borders (why metal made concrete). **[keep general; touchstone/weighing well attested.]**

### 03 — Money Among Strangers
- **slug:** `03-money-among-strangers`
- **summary:** Where there is no shared sovereign, no shared law, and no ongoing relationship, a thing with intrinsic acceptability does work an IOU cannot, the commodity theory's strongest ground.
- **objectives:**
  - Explain why cross-border and stranger-to-stranger trade is the commodity theory's strongest empirical territory.
  - Explain why the credit theory is weakest where no relationship or sovereign anchors a debt.
  - State the standing objection that any credit/state theory of money must answer.
- **prereqs:** 02
- **est. minutes:** 26
- **notes:** Concede this honestly and at full strength; it is the real objection the credit theory must answer later. This is part of the commodity steelman, given its own room because it is the metallist's best empirical pillar. Owns: stranger/cross-border trade as the metallist's ground. Closing story: cowrie-shell currencies in the Atlantic slave trade, shells carried from the Maldives to West Africa as money among societies with no shared sovereign, a brutal concrete test of "what is money." **[verify the Maldives→West Africa cowrie route; broad fact established, keep specifics rough; handle with sobriety.]**

### 04 — Money as Credit, Money as the State's Word
- **slug:** `04-credit-and-the-state`
- **summary:** Six thinkers are usually mashed into "the chartalists," and the module's whole job is to keep their distinct claims apart.
- **objectives:**
  - Distinguish Innes (money is credit), Knapp (the state names money), Keynes (money of account is primary), and Lerner (tax-acceptance gives money value) as four different claims.
  - Explain Grierson's argument that the abstract unit of account originates in customary compensation tariffs (wergild), not market haggling.
  - Summarize Ingham's sociological synthesis of money as a social relation and instituted unit of account.
- **prereqs:** 01
- **est. minutes:** 32
- **notes:** The credit/state column's steelman, and the course's chief integrity test: **do not blend the thinkers** (Innes ≠ Knapp ≠ Keynes ≠ Lerner ≠ Grierson ≠ Ingham, each a distinct claim about what makes money money). Lay them side by side with a compact who-claims-what structure. Lerner is the bridge *toward* (not into) MMT, fenced for module 9. Owns: Innes/credit, Knapp/state-naming, Keynes/money-of-account, Lerner/tax-drives-value, Grierson/wergild, Ingham/synthesis. Closing story: A. Mitchell Innes the outsider, a diplomat who published the credit theory in the *Banking Law Journal* (1913, 1914), was ignored, and was revived decades later. **[Innes's essays and revival established; exact official posts [verify].]**

### 05 — The Silver Problem
- **slug:** `05-the-silver-problem`
- **summary:** The Mesopotamian standard was denominated in silver, a commodity, so money-of-account before coins does not by itself refute the commodity theory, and the answer is that the standard was set by law, not by a market.
- **objectives:**
  - State the flagship objection: a silver standard is a commodity standard, so unit-of-account-before-coins does not refute metallism on its own.
  - Explain the instituted/administered-standard reply (silver/barley ratios, prices, and interest set by temple, palace, and Hammurabi's code; the shekel as a unit of weight; the temple as great creditor) and Polanyi's separable money-uses.
  - Evaluate whether the instituted-standard point settles the question or only shifts it, giving the metallist rejoinder its full weight.
- **prereqs:** 02, 03, 04
- **est. minutes:** 34
- **notes:** **THE HINGE. Protect at all costs; never compress.** This is where the two theories built in 02–04 collide on one body of evidence, and where "sharper *and* more qualified" is demonstrated rather than asserted. Build it as: objection at full strength (the silver is a commodity) → the administered-standard reply (Hammurabi's ratios and interest caps read for the *prices*, not just "debt was law"; the shekel as weight; temple/palace as creditors; Polanyi's separable uses) → the honest re-opening (silver was *chosen* for its commodity properties; an administered ratio still rides on a real commodity's value), landing on **shifts, not settles. No winner.** Enact the evidence/interpretation seam by structure (the tablet as evidence; each theory's reading as a named, attributed beat; the rejoinder a third beat), never by reciting it. Owns: the silver problem, the instituted/administered standard, the shekel-as-weight, Polanyi's separable money-uses, "shifts not settles." Closing story: a temple loan tablet readable two ways, the same weighed-silver loan at an administered ratio that a metallist and a chartalist each claim. **[representative attested practice; do NOT invent a specific tablet, lender, rate, or accession number.]**

### 06 — Coins, Soldiers, and Taxes
- **slug:** `06-coins-soldiers-and-taxes`
- **summary:** The first coins are a datable, state-stamped fact, and a bolder causal story about armies, taxes, and slaves is Graeber's reading, not the chronology.
- **objectives:**
  - State the solid chronology of the first coins (~7th c. BCE, Lydia and Ionian Greece, electrum, state-stamped).
  - Explain Graeber's "military-coinage complex" as an interpretation of how coinage spread, attributed to him.
  - Distinguish the solid co-occurrence of coin, soldiers, and taxes from the contested strong causal claim, and name the critics.
- **prereqs:** 05
- **est. minutes:** 28
- **notes:** Recall (one clause) that coins are late from the intro course; here weigh the *causation*. Solid fact (coin + soldiers + taxes entangled) stated as its own beat; the strong **military-coinage(-slavery) complex** named as **Graeber's** reading with critics who find it over-drawn and regionally uneven. The solid-fact-then-attributed-interpretation shape is the module's spine (enacted, not recited). Owns: the coin chronology (recall + sharpen), the military-coinage complex as Graeber's interpretation. Closing story: the early electrum finds at the Artemision (temple of Artemis) at Ephesus, the oldest coins under a temple floor (the date-shock embodied); alt for the interpretation beat, Alexander minting captured Persian bullion to pay troops. **[verify the Artemision attribution/dating; fall back to "~7th c. BCE Lydia/Ionia electrum" if uncertain.]**

### 07 — Debt, Interest, and the Clean Slate
- **slug:** `07-debt-and-the-clean-slate`
- **summary:** Interest-bearing debt is older than coin, and the royal debt cancellations were real, but how regular and far-reaching they were is contested.
- **objectives:**
  - Explain that measured, enforceable, interest-bearing debt predates coinage, and the political danger of debt bondage.
  - Summarize Hudson's case that royal clean-slate decrees (misharum/andurarum) and the biblical Jubilee were recurrent tools against debt peonage.
  - Distinguish the attested existence of these decrees from the contested claim about their frequency, enforcement, and reach.
- **prereqs:** 05
- **est. minutes:** 30
- **notes:** **Steelman Hudson first** (the decrees are real and attested; the institution mattered), **then qualify** (frequency/enforcement/scope contested; some specialists read him as over-generalizing). Attested institution vs. contested thesis, kept distinct by structure. Don't romanticize (debt bondage was real). Primary-source slot: Leviticus 25 on the Jubilee, or the Code of Hammurabi on interest/debt. Owns: interest-bearing-debt-before-coin, clean-slate cancellations (Hudson, steelmanned then qualified). Closing story: Solon's *seisachtheia* (~594 BCE Athens), the "shaking off of burdens" that cancelled debts, freed those enslaved for debt, and barred debt-slavery, a clean-slate outside Mesopotamia near the birth of coinage. **[well established; keep the date rough, no invented figures/quotations.]**

### 08 — The Debt That Founds the World
- **slug:** `08-the-debt-that-founds-the-world`
- **summary:** The boldest claim in the field says society itself rests on an original debt to gods or ancestors, and it is exactly where an attractive reading outruns the evidence.
- **objectives:**
  - State the primordial-debt thesis (society rests on a founding debt from which money and tax descend) and attribute it to its theorists.
  - Explain why it is widely judged an untestable over-reach that reintroduces a theory-required origin story.
  - Apply the course's qualification discipline: take a bold, influential thesis and show precisely why the evidence cannot bear it, without sneering.
- **prereqs:** 07
- **est. minutes:** 26
- **notes:** The course's cleanest *qualification* lesson. Present the **primordial-debt** thesis (Aglietta/Orléan/Théret; taken up by Graeber) as **genuine, attributed, and over-reaching**, not as the frame. The throughline back to the intro course: an unfalsifiable origin story is exactly what the reader was taught to distrust. Steelman it honestly first (its intuitive and textual pull), then mark precisely where it outruns the evidence. Owns: the primordial-debt thesis and its critique. Closing story: the Vedic/Brahmanic "debt to the gods" texts the theorists draw on, told as the *source material* of the thesis and the point where universal history outruns the record. **[verify the specific Vedic "debts" schema before stating precisely; present as the theorists' source, not established universal history.]**

### 09 — The Money in Your Account
- **slug:** `09-the-money-in-your-account`
- **summary:** Modern money is created when banks lend, a central bank says so, and the contrast with metal-backed money is where the ancient argument meets the balance in your account, kept clear of policy.
- **objectives:**
  - Explain endogenous credit money ("loans create deposits") and how the Bank of England's 2014 account reversed the textbook money-multiplier story.
  - Contrast how the commodity theory (via Mises's regression) and the credit/state theory each handle fiat money.
  - Distinguish chartalism (a theory of money's nature) from Modern Monetary Theory (a present-day policy program), and state the relationship without taking a policy position.
- **prereqs:** 04
- **est. minutes:** 30
- **notes:** **Bounded and fenced.** Three things only: credit money / "loans create deposits" (BoE 2014); fiat and the commodity theory's modern strain (Mises's regression to a now-severed commodity link vs. the chartalist taking fiat as normal); and **one fenced passage** naming **chartalism ≠ MMT** (theory of money's nature vs. policy program). **No policy advocacy** anywhere; this is an integrity checkpoint. State credit money as *how modern money works*, not as proof the credit theory wins the *origin* debate. Owns: endogenous/credit money, "loans create deposits," fiat's modern strain, the chartalism≠MMT fence. Closing story: the Bank of England publishing *Money creation in the modern economy* in 2014, a central bank stating in plain words that the textbook was wrong and that loans create deposits. **[real, citable by title/year; safe.]**

### 10 — What Is Settled, What Is Open
- **slug:** `10-settled-and-open`
- **summary:** An honest ledger of what the evidence has settled and what it has not, and a method for weighing the next confident claim about what money really is.
- **objectives:**
  - Distinguish what is genuinely settled (the barter sequence is dead; coins are late and state-stamped; money-of-account is ancient; banks create credit money) from what is genuinely open (metallism vs. chartalism on money's nature; whether the silver problem shifts or settles; coinage causation; Hudson's scope; the primordial-debt over-reach).
  - Apply the method: separate evidence from interpretation, ask which theory a piece of evidence actually bears on and how far, steelman before judging.
  - Defend the position that some of this is productively unsettled rather than awaiting a single true answer.
- **prereqs:** 01-09
- **est. minutes:** 26
- **notes:** The close and payoff; should feel like recognition. An honest settled/open ledger, then the method, one level up from the intro course's "remembered, or required by a theory?": **"which theory does this evidence bear on, and how far?"** Refuse the verdict the field refuses; model tolerating an honest "unsettled." Owns: the settled/open ledger, the adjudication method. Closing story: Keynes's serious, years-long work on ancient monetary standards and money-of-account that fed the *Treatise*, a first-rank mind living inside exactly this unsettled question and not resolving it cleanly. **[verify the "Babylonian madness" phrasing/source; if uncertain, use the solid fact that Keynes worked on ancient standards for the *Treatise* and keep it general.]**

- **Chartalism ≠ MMT:** named once and fenced in module 9; no policy advocacy anywhere (an editorial/verify checkpoint).
- **No fabrication:** rough dates; no invented clause numbers, rates, etymologies, accession numbers, or quotations; primary quotations accurate or labelled paraphrase; `[verify]` specifics resolved or kept general by authoring.
- **Continuity:** reuse the intro canon's glosses for shared terms verbatim; recall, never re-derive; the course feels like the advanced sequel from page one.
- **Closing stories:** each module ends in a fact-dense, verifiable, points-true story; varied across the course; Yap stones excluded (intro course's territory).
