The Essentials primer. Owns the series-canon: the diagnosis, the policy toolkit, the NZ-context facts,
and the six shared metaphors. One idea-cluster: **TOP's policies all answer one diagnosis — NZ taxes
work and spending but barely touches land and capital — so to understand any TOP policy you need the
diagnosis, a small toolkit of concepts, and the NZ facts.**

## Modules

### M1 — The Diagnosis · slug `01-the-diagnosis` · ~14 min
- **summary:** What TOP thinks is broken in New Zealand — one connected story that every TOP policy answers.
- **objectives:** (1) State TOP's core diagnosis (the tax/economy rewards owning land over working or building a business). (2) Explain how housing became NZ's default investment and what that does to the young and to wealth concentration. (3) Connect the cost-of-living squeeze and "pendulum politics" to the same story.
- **owns (shared):** the diagnosis; the **scales tilted toward property over work** metaphor; the attribution habit ("TOP argues…").
- **prereqs:** none.

### M2 — The Policy Toolkit · slug `02-the-policy-toolkit` · ~16 min
- **summary:** The five or six concepts you need to follow any TOP policy — defined in plain words, once.
- **objectives:** (1) Define **tax base**, **land value tax** (the spot, not the building), **Citizen's Income / UBI**, **economic distortion/deadweight**, **means-testing**, **tax incidence**. (2) Explain why TOP argues an LVT barely distorts behaviour ("land can't run away"). (3) Distinguish a **floor** (unconditional) from a **safety net** (means-tested) and note that a floor's height matters.
- **owns (shared):** land value tax, Citizen's Income, distortion, means-testing, incidence; the **tax-as-signpost**, **spot-not-house**, **land-can't-run-away**, and **floor-vs-safety-net** metaphors.
- **prereqs:** M1.

### M3 — The NZ Context, and a Map of the Platform · slug `03-nz-context-and-the-map` · ~15 min
- **summary:** The New Zealand facts the policies lean on — and a one-page map of everything TOP is proposing.
- **objectives:** (1) State the key NZ-context facts (no comprehensive CGT; GST 15%; the IRD effective-rate gap; housing unaffordability and wealth concentration). (2) Explain why these facts make TOP's policies "fit" NZ. (3) Map the whole 2026 platform (Tax Reset; homes/energy/oceans; democracy/youth/everyday life) so the reader knows what each sibling course covers.
- **owns (shared):** the NZ-context facts backbone; the **pendulum politics** metaphor; the platform map.
- **prereqs:** M1, M2.
