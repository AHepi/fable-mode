## A. Trim (the biggest job)

Every module ran long (authored bodies ~2,600–3,700 words). The course-design ceiling is
~1,000–2,500 words of *body prose*. The editorial cut removed within-module repetition, throat-clearing,
Context sections that re-developed what the Narrative then covered, and restatement past first strong
statement — while protecting the load-bearing beats (steelmans, named live debates, the
evidence/interpretation seams, the primary-source blocks, the distributed "Check yourself" checks, and
the discussion prompts + rubrics).

collapsible answer reveals), which is the figure the ceiling governs:

the primitive-accumulation section) was reduced to a callback on its second appearance.

## C. Terms, attributions, voice

- **Thinker first-mentions** land in their owner modules with the canon one-clause identifier and are
  *re-mentioned, not re-introduced* later: Marx (full in 04 → light in 09/10), Smith (full in 03 →
  light in 07/08), Polanyi (full in 02 → "Polanyi again" in 06), Thatcher (01 → "her again" in 11).
  Mauss/Sahlins (02), Graeber (03), Williams (05), Thompson/Macpherson (08), Federici (09),
  Gramsci/Foucault (10), Fisher/Mirowski/Slobodian (11) all carry their canon glosses.
- **Glosses** match the canon registry (embeddedness, the gift/reciprocity, commodity, fictitious
  commodities, self-regulating market, work-discipline, possessive individualism, social reproduction,
  commodity fetishism, reification, hegemony, discipline, neoliberalism, capitalist realism).
- **Voice levelled** toward the canon target (sharp essayist, one intelligent friend): history modules
  (04–09) pulled off any textbook dryness, theory modules (01, 10) pulled off abstraction toward the
  concrete (the shelf, the bell, the price tag). De-soothed: `lint-prose.mjs` sections 5–6 are clean
  (no hand-holding phrases; soothing manner-adverbs all within cap). The residual `simply/merely`
  minimizers are argumentative ("simply wrong", "not merely X but Y"), not soothing verb-crutches.

## D. Cross-module de-duplication (canon ledger)

No fact owned by one module is re-derived elsewhere; later uses are re-glossed callbacks. The three
canon-flagged integrity links are confirmed present and correct:

- **08 → 02:** the acquisitiveness counter cashes out via a callback to module 02's gift/reciprocity
  ("this counter is a *callback*, not a fresh argument"), not re-argued.
- **11 → 06:** "laissez-faire was planned" (06) and neoliberalism-as-project (11) are framed as the
  same denaturalizing move two centuries apart — 11 calls 06 back explicitly and develops it.
- **10 → 01:** reification, previewed in 01, is paid off in 10 ("cash in something else module 01
  slipped into your back pocket: its preview of reification. This is its full content"); 10's hook also
  pays off 01's price-tag check.

## E. Bridge sweep (intuition → concept seams)

Every specialist term is reached from an everyday intuition before it does work; no concept arrives as
a cliff. The two highest-risk **framing-shift** seams were verified as *constructed*, not asserted:

- **06, fictitious commodities → reification:** the module builds the intuition (land/labour/money were
  never made for sale, yet the market prices them as goods) and shows the mechanism — pricing
  non-commodities forces them to *appear* as things with a market rate — *before* naming that
  hardening-into-things move as reification. The naming arrives with the demonstration.
- **10, price tag → "a relation between things":** reached from 01's coffee-on-the-shelf and the
  single-commodity case, then generalized to the whole social world ("the market demands" as a
  thunderhead) before "second nature" is named. The Foucault-vs-ideology distinction is constructed
  (why "mask" is the wrong metaphor — the self that would do the unmasking was produced too), not
  asserted, and the three thinkers are kept un-blended per canon.

## Flags / notes for the gate

1. **Two remaining `lint-prose` HARD flags, both justified — not defects.** The lint caps any
   "image word" at 5 uses per module. The two over-cap words are the modules' **owned central running
   images** from the canon metaphor map: `machine` (6×) in module 06 ("the market as a machine someone
   built") and `room` (9×) in module 11 ("the room built so the walls can't be seen"). These are
   developed, paid-off central metaphors — exactly the recurrence editorial-craft asks of an image that
   carries a module's spine — not decorative repetition. Both were trimmed from their authored levels
   (06: 9→6; 11: 16→9) by varying the non-load-bearing instances to "walls / built engine / it"; the
   remainder are load-bearing. I judged further cuts would damage the paid-off image rather than help.
2. **Em-dash density is SOFT-flagged in all eleven** (15–23 per 1,000 words vs the ~12 advisory).
   This is a real house-wide voice tic. It is advisory, not a gate; I did light reduction during the
   pass but did not chase every dash, to avoid over-smoothing the voice (literary-maverick caution).
   Flagging for the human reader in case a heavier dash-thinning pass is wanted, especially in 07 and 11.
3. **No suspect quotations found.** All quoted passages are well-established anchors named in the canon
   quotation rule; none was reworded or given an invented page number. The authors' own confidence
   notes (e.g. Federici parenthetical, Polanyi "confident of wording, no page number") were left intact.
4. **`estimatedMinutes` lowered** to track the trims: 01 26→24, 02 28→26, 03 24 (unchanged), 04 30→27,
   05 28→26, 06 30→28, 07 28 (unchanged), 08 28→26, 09 28 (unchanged), 10 30→27, 11 32→29. Course total
