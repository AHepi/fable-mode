## Modules

### M1 — Citizens' Voice and Cleaner Politics · slug `01-citizens-voice` · ~15 min
- **summary:** Breaking gridlock with citizens' assemblies — and TOP's anti-corruption clean-up.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the mechanism (randomly selected, paid, representative **citizens' assemblies** that study a gridlocked issue and make recommendations government must respond to; a new Parliamentary Commissioner; plus a lobbying register and donation limits). (2) Explain the problem ("pendulum politics" waste and gridlock on Super, housing, climate). (3) Explain the NZ fit (the short 3-year cycle; Ireland/France models) and the critiques (assemblies are advisory; risk of being ignored or captured).
- **owns (course-local):** citizens' assemblies; clean-up politics. **Calls back:** pendulum politics (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** none.

### M2 — The Teal Card · slug `02-the-teal-card` · ~14 min
- **summary:** A bundle of support for under-30s — health, transport, and a savings boost tied to civic service.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the mechanism (free GP/dental/eye/mental-health care under 30; free public transport; a bike credit; a $5,000 savings boost at 18 unlocked by a National Civic Service). (2) Explain the problem (youth health gaps, cost barriers, an insecure start). (3) Give the critiques (cost; tying money to mandatory service; the age cut-off's fairness).
- **owns (course-local):** the Teal Card components; National Civic Service. **Calls back:** the cost-of-living diagnosis (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** M1.

### M3 — Getting Around, and Drug Law · slug `03-transport-and-drugs` · ~14 min
- **summary:** Two everyday-life policies — permanent free public transport (paid for by road-user charges) and a harm-reduction approach to drugs.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the transport mechanism (permanent free public transport; road-user charging to replace fuel tax; long-horizon infrastructure). (2) Explain the drug-reform mechanism (harm minimisation; legalise cannabis; decriminalise small quantities; fund kaupapa Māori addiction services). (3) Give the problems targeted and the main critiques for each (who pays for free transport; the public-safety drug debate and the 2020 referendum result).
- **owns (course-local):** transport/RUC; drug reform. **Calls back:** the diagnosis (cost of living).
- **prereqs:** M1, M2.
