Not every policy is about money. Some are about *how the country decides* — and some are about the
texture of ordinary life: getting to work, going to the dentist at twenty-four, what the law does
about drugs. This short course gathers the rest of The Opportunity Party's platform under one roof.

The first module covers **Citizens' Voice and cleaner politics** — TOP's plan to break "pendulum
politics" with randomly chosen citizens' assemblies, plus a lobbying register and donation limits. The
second covers the **Teal Card**, a bundle of free healthcare, transport and a savings boost for
under-30s, tied to a civic-service scheme. The third covers two everyday-life policies that often
surprise people: **permanent free public transport** (paid for by road-user charges) and a
**harm-reduction** approach to drugs, including cannabis legalisation.

These are contested policies, so the course is especially careful to stay even-handed: each is
explained on its own terms and attributed to TOP, and each is paired with its strongest objections —
the cost of free fares, the fairness of tying money to mandatory service, the public-safety arguments
around drug reform and the 2020 referendum that narrowly rejected legalising cannabis. As ever, the
aim is to leave you able to discuss them, not to tell you what to think.
