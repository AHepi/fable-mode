# Canon — Brewing a Better Cup of Coffee

The consistency contract. **Binding** on every authoring sub-agent and on the editorial stage. Its
job is to stop the three blind-parallel authors from drifting apart.

## 1. Symbol & naming registry

One name for every recurring thing. Use exactly these; do not invent synonyms.

| Thing | Canonical name | Not |
|-------|----------------|-----|
| Dissolving flavour out of coffee into water | **extraction** (verb: *extract*) | "pulling flavour", "drawing out", "leaching", "brewing out" |
| The ground coffee itself | **the grounds** | "grinds", "the bed", "the coffee" (for the solid) |
| Coffee-to-water mass ratio | **brew ratio** | "dose ratio", "coffee-to-water ratio" as a term, "strength setting" |
| A cup that tasted sharp/sour because too little was extracted | **under-extracted** | "sour cup", "weak", "watery" (as the diagnosis) |
| A cup that tasted harsh/bitter because too much was extracted | **over-extracted** | "burnt", "too strong", "bitter cup" (as the diagnosis) |
| The four changeable inputs | **levers** (grind, brew ratio, water, time) | "knobs", "factors", "variables", "settings" (in prose) |

## 2. "Already-taught" ledger & dedup plan

Each shared fact has **one owner** that gives the full treatment; every later use is a one-line
**callback**, never a re-derivation.

| Fact | Owner (full treatment) | Callback in | Suggested callback line |
|------|------------------------|-------------|-------------------------|
| What **extraction** is (solubles dissolving over time; fast acids → sugars → bitter last) | **M1** | M2, M3 | "extraction — the dissolving-over-time idea from module 1" |
| The **taste compass** (sour = under-extracted, bitter = over-extracted, sweet spot between) | **M1** | M2, M3 | "remember the compass: sour means under, bitter means over" |
| The **four levers** defined (grind / brew ratio / water / time and their direction) | **M2** | M3 | "the four levers from module 2" |

## 3. Metaphor-ownership map

One running image per module; others reference, they don't reinvent. (Ownership is not endorsement —
each must point at one correct referent and be paid off.)

| Image | Owner module | Why it points true |
|-------|--------------|--------------------|
| Extraction as **rinsing/steeping** — water washes flavour off the grounds, the easy stuff first | M1 | maps to solubles dissolving in time order; no misleading second meaning |
| A bad cup as a reading on the **taste compass** (a needle between *sour* and *bitter*) | M1 | one axis, one needle; pays off as the diagnostic in M3 |
| Dialing in as **tuning an old radio** — small turns toward the clear station | M3 | continuous adjustment toward a target; no chance/gambling association |

> NB: do **not** describe the levers as a "slot machine" or anything with a gambling/random reading —
> extraction is deterministic. (This is the canon's standing warning per literary-maverick.)

## 4. Stock-phrase caps

| Phrase | Cap | Keeper |
|--------|-----|--------|
| "the perfect cup" | 1 total | M1 |
| "dial(ing) in" as the *named method* | M3 only | M3 |
| marketing gush — "elevate your coffee", "flavour journey", "barista-level", "game-changer" | 0 (banned) | — |
| empty openers — "Let's dive in", "Let's get started", "In this module we will" | 0 (banned) | — |

## 5. Voice target

One narrator across all three: **a warm, plain-spoken friend who happens to know coffee cold.** Second
person ("you"), short concrete sentences, hands-on not encyclopedic, dry humour allowed, marketing
gush forbidden. Calibrate to a total beginner — gloss every term on first use.

**Drift to watch for** (correct in editorial): a module that turns *clinical/encyclopedic* (lists of
numbers, passive voice) gets pulled toward the hands-on friend; a module that turns *purple* about
"ritual" and "artisanal craft" gets reined back to plain.
