# Curriculum — Brewing a Better Cup of Coffee

**kind:** skill · **level:** intro · **estimatedHours:** ~1 · **modules:** 3 (mini-course, audit run)

**Description (brand voice):** Stop making coffee by luck. Learn the one idea that explains every cup
you've ever brewed — and the four knobs that fix the bad ones.

## Prerequisite graph
M1 → M2 → M3. Each uses only earlier modules. No forward references.

## Module specs

### 01 — What Brewing Actually Is  (slug: `what-brewing-is`)
- **Summary:** Brewing is *extraction* — dissolving flavour out of grounds over time — and where you
  stop on that timeline is what your cup tastes like.
- **Objectives:** Explain extraction in one sentence; identify a cup as under- or over-extracted from
  its taste; distinguish strength from extraction.
- **Owns (canon):** the concept of *extraction*; the *taste compass* (sour = under, bitter = over);
  the steeping/rinsing image.
- **Prereqs:** none. **est:** 12 min.

### 02 — The Four Levers  (slug: `the-levers`)
- **Summary:** Four things a home brewer can change — grind, brew ratio, water, time — and which way
  each pushes extraction.
- **Objectives:** Name the four levers; state the direction each moves extraction; pick the right
  lever for a described problem.
- **Owns (canon):** the *four levers* and their definitions. **Calls back** to extraction + the taste
  compass (M1) — must NOT re-derive them.
- **Prereqs:** M1. **est:** 15 min.

### 03 — Dialing In  (slug: `dialing-in`)
- **Summary:** Diagnose a bad cup from its taste and fix it by moving one lever at a time toward the
  centre of the compass.
- **Objectives:** Diagnose a cup as under/over-extracted; choose a single corrective lever; explain
  why changing one lever at a time is the whole discipline.
- **Owns (canon):** the *one-lever-at-a-time* method; the tuning-a-radio image. **Calls back** to
  extraction, the taste compass (M1) and the four levers (M2).
- **Prereqs:** M1, M2. **est:** 15 min.
