## Members

A series of mini-courses explaining the 2026 policy platform of **The Opportunity Party (TOP)**. The
**Essentials** course is authored first and owns the shared canon (`runs/top-policies-20260615/series-canon.md`):
the diagnosis, the policy toolkit (land value tax, Citizen's Income, tax base, distortion), and the NZ
context every other course reuses. `seriesSlug: top-2026` is the `/series/top-2026` route.

| slug | title | role | order | one-line scope |

| top-essentials | "TOP 2026: The Essentials" | essentials | 1 | the diagnosis, the policy toolkit, and the NZ context the whole platform leans on |
| the-tax-reset | "The Tax Reset" | course | 2 | the flagship: Citizen's Income, the Land Value Tax, and who wins, who loses, and the critiques |

## Authoring order & ownership

- **TOP 2026: The Essentials** is produced first; its curriculum writes `runs/top-policies-20260615/series-canon.md`.
- The three siblings then run the pipeline, reading the series-canon as a binding input: they reuse the
  shared terms/metaphors verbatim and **call back** to Essentials for the diagnosis and toolkit (land
  value tax, Citizen's Income, distortion, the NZ tax gaps) rather than re-deriving them.

## Interpretation notes

- **This is a real political party.** The series EXPLAINS TOP's platform faithfully but stays neutral:
  attribute positions and especially TOP's own modelling ("TOP argues/claims…"); mark independent facts
  (IRD effective-rate data, Stats NZ housing data) as such; and **pair every policy with its strongest
  critiques** — the reader's goal is to *discuss*, not parrot. The course never tells the reader to
  support or oppose anything.
- **No history lesson.** Party founding/leaders/"cat policy" are out of scope; the 1989 tax switch and
  NZ's old land tax appear only as one-line context for why a policy fits NZ.
- Genuinely shared (live in the series-canon): the diagnosis; tax base; land value tax; Citizen's
  Income / UBI; economic distortion/deadweight; means-testing; tax incidence; the NZ context facts; and
  the six shared metaphors. Course-local specifics (e.g. "bottom trawling", "citizens' assembly",
  "Teal Card") belong to one mini.
- Each sibling must stand alone: a reader who starts with "Homes, Energy and Oceans" should follow it
  via one-line callbacks to Essentials, not be blocked by them.
- **Currency caveat:** 2025–2026 platform figures; flag `[verify]` items (exact LVT rate under new
  leadership, income-tax thresholds, the ~900k Citizen's-Income eligibility gap, deferral interest,
  Māori-land treatment).
