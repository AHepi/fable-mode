# Series-Canon — The Opportunity Party's 2026 Policies

The **series** consistency contract. Written by **TOP 2026: The Essentials** at its curriculum stage,
**binding** on all three sibling mini-courses. One vocabulary, one set of metaphors, one neutral voice
across the whole series. Decisive — one call per item.

> **Relationship to each member's `canon.md`.** This governs only the **shared** surface (the
> diagnosis, the toolkit, the NZ-context facts, and the metaphors). Each mini still writes its own
> `canon.md` for internal consistency and may add course-local terms (e.g. "bottom trawling",
> "citizens' assembly", "Teal Card"). On a shared concept, the series-canon wins.

## 1. Shared TERMS registry

One name per shared concept, reused **verbatim** by every member.

| Shared concept | Canonical name | Notes |
|----------------|----------------|-------|
| the party | **The Opportunity Party (TOP)** | note **once per course** that it was "The Opportunities Party"; thereafter "TOP". |
| what gets taxed | **tax base** | TOP's core move: shift the base toward land. |
| TOP's signature tax | **land value tax (LVT)** | annual tax on the *unimproved value of land* — the spot, not the building. Always spell out on first use, then "LVT". |
| TOP's basic-income payment | **Citizen's Income** | TOP's version of a UBI; capitalise. |
| the generic idea | **UBI (universal basic income)** | the family of ideas the Citizen's Income belongs to. |
| profit on selling an asset | **capital gain**; **capital gains tax (CGT)** | NZ largely doesn't tax it — a recurring fact. |
| how taxes bend behaviour/waste value | **economic distortion** (a.k.a. **deadweight**) | LVT's central claim: near-zero distortion. |
| limiting a payment by income | **means-testing** | TOP removes it for the Citizen's Income; *adds* it to Super. |
| who really bears a tax | **tax incidence** (a.k.a. **pass-through**) | e.g. do landlords pass LVT to tenants? |
| TOP's flagship package | **the Tax Reset** | Citizen's Income + LVT + income-tax/KiwiSaver. |
| TOP's self-positioning | **"radical centrism" / "blue-green"** | always in quotes (it's their label, and contested). |

Attribution words to keep consistent: **"TOP proposes / argues / claims"** for the party's positions
and modelling; **"according to [Stats NZ / IRD / the IMF]"** for independent facts.

## 2. Shared METAPHOR map

The running images the whole series reuses. A sibling **references** these; it does not reinvent them.
All six are established in **TOP 2026: The Essentials**.

| Image / metaphor | What it stands for | Guard (must NOT imply) | First established in |
|------------------|--------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Scales tilted toward property over work** | the effective-rate gap — the system rewards owning over earning | it's about *structure/incentives*, not that property owners are villains | Essentials, M1 |
| **A tax is a signpost (a toll that changes which road people take)** | taxes steer behaviour (incentives/distortion) | not all taxes steer equally — the LVT claim is it *barely* steers | Essentials, M2 |
| **Taxing the worth of the spot, not the house on it** | the LVT base (unimproved land value) | it does NOT tax your building/renovations; not a tax on "having a home" | Essentials, M2 |
| **Land can't run away** | land as an immobile tax base (unlike income/capital) | it's the *value of the location*, not the dirt; "can't flee" doesn't make a big bill painless | Essentials, M2 |
| **A floor you stand on vs. a safety net with holes** | the Citizen's Income as an unconditional base vs. means-tested welfare | a floor's *height* matters — TOP's floor is low; it doesn't by itself lift everyone to comfort | Essentials, M2 |
| **Pendulum politics** | NZ's lurch-and-reverse electoral cycle (the case for Citizens' Voice) | not all change is waste; some reversal is healthy democracy | Essentials, M3 |

**Ownership is not endorsement.** Each image points at one correct referent and carries its guard. The
two highest-stakes guards: the **floor's height matters** (don't let "everyone gets a floor" imply
"everyone is now fine"), and **scales** is about structure, not blame.

## 3. Shared VOICE target

One calm, even-handed **explainer** — a sharp friend walking you through the policies so you can hold
your own at a dinner-table argument. Plain words, concrete NZ examples first (rates, rent, a $1M
Auckland section, a KiwiSaver balance), jargon glossed on the spot. **Scrupulously neutral**: attribute
TOP's claims to TOP, attribute facts to their source, and **always follow a policy's rationale with its
strongest critique** — then let the reader decide. Never campaigns, cheerleads, or sneers ("you should
back/reject this" is banned). Curious, fair, a little dry; respects the reader as a smart adult who
wants the mechanics, not a verdict.

**Beginner-first (non-negotiable, especially for the Essentials course):** assume the reader knows no
economics or tax. Define every term in plain words — with an everyday example — the moment it first
appears; lead with intuition before vocabulary; keep paragraphs short and one idea at a time; prefer
fewer words. Never open on a dense concept like "capital gains tax." A term used before it is explained
is a defect, not a style choice.
