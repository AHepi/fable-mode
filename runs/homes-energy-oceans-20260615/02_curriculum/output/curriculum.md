## Modules

### M1 — Affordable Housing · slug `01-affordable-housing` · ~15 min
- **summary:** Beyond the land tax — TOP's supply-side plan to build more homes and bring prices down.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the non-tax housing levers (simpler national zoning; easier consenting for tiny homes/sleepouts; a new Urban Development Act; government underwriting to smooth the boom-bust cycle). (2) Explain the problem (an ~80,000-home shortage; the developed world's worst affordability). (3) Give the main critiques (upzoning opposition; falling prices hurt recent buyers; supply is slow). 
- **owns (course-local):** zoning/consenting/Urban Development Act. **Calls back:** the land value tax as the *demand-side* lever, and the housing diagnosis (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** none.

### M2 — Abundant Energy · slug `02-abundant-energy` · ~15 min
- **summary:** A 25-year energy plan to triple renewable capacity — and treat energy as the engine of a productive economy.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the mechanism (a bipartisan 25-year strategy; ~300% of current renewable capacity / ~30 GW by 2050; household electrification assistance; overbuild wind/solar and hold hydro as backup). (2) Explain the problem (decades of under-investment; price spikes; business uncertainty). (3) Explain the NZ fit (already ~80–85% renewable) and the critiques (capital cost; ambition; bipartisanship is hard).
- **owns (course-local):** the energy targets/mechanism. **Calls back:** "environment as economic policy" framing.
- **prereqs:** M1.

### M3 — Healthy Oceans · slug `03-healthy-oceans` · ~14 min
- **summary:** Protect a third of the ocean, end bottom trawling, and grow a "blue economy" — environment framed as jobs.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the mechanism (protect 30% of the ocean by 2035, up from ~0.5%; phase out bottom trawling with worker transition; invest in aquaculture; an Ocean's Secretariat). (2) Explain the problem (tiny marine protection; declining stocks; an under-used blue economy). (3) Explain the NZ fit (one of the world's largest ocean zones; Treaty fishing-rights protections) and the critiques (fishing-industry cost; enforcement over a vast area).
- **owns (course-local):** marine protection / bottom trawling / blue economy. **Calls back:** the "environment as economy" framing.
- **prereqs:** M1, M2.
