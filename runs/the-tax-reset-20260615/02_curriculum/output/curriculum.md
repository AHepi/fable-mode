## Modules

### M1 — A Citizen's Income · slug `01-the-citizens-income` · ~15 min
- **summary:** What TOP's Citizen's Income actually is, the problem it targets, and why a poverty-level floor is the heart of the debate.
- **objectives:** (1) Describe the mechanism (≈$370/week to almost all adults; near-unconditional; replaces most welfare and NZ Super with transitional top-ups). (2) Explain the problem it targets (welfare "abatement traps", complexity, cost of living). (3) Lay out the main critiques (adequacy for disabled people; replacing universal Super; the ~900k eligibility gap).
- **owns (course-local):** the Citizen's Income's specifics; Child Support Income. **Calls back:** UBI / floor-vs-safety-net (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** none.

### M2 — The Land Value Tax · slug `02-the-land-value-tax` · ~16 min
- **summary:** How TOP would tax the value of land to help fund the Citizen's Income — plus the income-tax and KiwiSaver changes that come with it.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain the LVT mechanism (1.75% urban land value / 0.5% rural; land not buildings; deferral for the cash-poor) and how it funds the Citizen's Income. (2) Explain the income-tax simplification (three brackets) and compulsory KiwiSaver 2.0, and why TOP bundles them. (3) Explain why TOP argues an LVT fits NZ (no CGT; existing land valuations; "land can't run away").
- **owns (course-local):** LVT rates/mechanics; KiwiSaver 2.0; income-tax brackets. **Calls back:** land value tax, distortion, the NZ tax gaps (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** M1.

### M3 — Winners, Losers and the Critiques · slug `03-winners-losers-critiques` · ~15 min
- **summary:** TOP's "70/20/10" claim, who actually gains and pays, and the strongest objections from left and right.
- **objectives:** (1) Explain TOP's distributional claim (70% better off / 20% unchanged / 10% pay more) and that it's TOP's own modelling. (2) Walk concrete cases (a renter; a young family; an asset-rich/cash-poor retiree; a high-value Auckland landowner). (3) Lay out the big critiques (asset-rich/cash-poor; taxes only one asset class; valuation difficulty; rent pass-through debate; Super replacement) so the reader can argue any side.
- **owns (course-local):** the 70/20/10 case and the package-level critiques; Pitfall-style "attribute the claim". **Calls back:** scales/incidence (Essentials).
- **prereqs:** M1, M2.

## Notes for authoring
- **Highest neutrality stakes in the series.** Attribute the 70/20/10 and revenue figures to TOP; give the critiques real weight; never tell the reader what to conclude.
