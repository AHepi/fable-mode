If you only learn one of The Opportunity Party's policies, learn this one. The **Tax Reset** is the
party's flagship — and its most radical bet: replace some of the tax New Zealand puts on *working* with
a tax on *owning land*, and pay almost every adult a guaranteed **Citizen's Income**. Pull the thread
and the whole platform comes with it: how the land tax pays for the income, why the income tax gets
simplified, why KiwiSaver becomes compulsory.

This short course takes the package apart, one piece at a time. The first module explains the
**Citizen's Income** — what it is, the welfare problems it targets, and why its modest size is the
heart of the argument. The second explains the **Land Value Tax** that helps fund it, plus the income
tax and KiwiSaver changes bundled alongside. The third is where it gets interesting: TOP's claim that
**seventy per cent of households end up better off**, who actually wins and loses, and the strongest
objections from both the left and the right.

It stands on its own — where it leans on a shared idea (a land tax barely changes behaviour because
"land can't run away"; a Citizen's Income is a floor everyone stands on), it reminds you in a line. As
throughout the series, TOP's own figures are attributed to TOP and the modelling is flagged as the
party's own; the goal is to leave you able to argue the package from any side.
