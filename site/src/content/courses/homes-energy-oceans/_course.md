The Opportunity Party calls itself "blue-green" — and three of its policies show what that means in
practice: a plan for **homes**, a plan for **energy**, and a plan for the **ocean**, all argued as
*economic* policy rather than charity or sentiment. Build enough houses and you fix affordability and
free up capital; build abundant clean power and you run a modern economy cheaply; protect the sea and
you grow the industries that depend on it.

This short course takes each in turn. The first module covers **affordable housing** — the zoning,
consenting and building reforms TOP proposes *beyond* its land tax. The second covers **abundant
energy** — a 25-year plan to triple renewable generation and treat power as the engine of growth. The
third covers **healthy oceans** — protecting a third of New Zealand's waters, ending bottom trawling,
and growing a "blue economy," with Treaty fishing rights in the frame.

Each module stands on its own and reminds you of any shared idea in a line. And as everywhere in this
series, the policies are explained, not sold: TOP's targets are attributed to TOP, and every plan is
weighed against its real critics — the homeowners who fear falling prices, the cost of a 25-year energy
build, the fishing communities a trawling ban would hit.
