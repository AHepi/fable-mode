Every election, a small party turns up with a stack of policies and a pitch that sounds either
visionary or mad, depending on who you ask. The Opportunity Party (TOP) — a minor New Zealand party
that styles itself "radical centrism" — is one of those. Its 2026 platform is unusually
*systematic*: a land tax, a guaranteed income, an energy plan, an ocean plan, a democracy fix. To
argue about any one of them at the pub, you first need the few ideas they all share.

That is what this short primer gives you. It is the **Essentials** course of a four-part series, built
for someone with no economics background who wants to *understand* these policies — well enough to
discuss them, not just nod along. Across three modules it lays out TOP's **diagnosis** of what's gone
wrong in New Zealand, the small **toolkit** of concepts its policies run on (what a land value tax is,
what a Citizen's Income is, why taxes "distort" behaviour), and the **New Zealand facts** — no capital
gains tax, sky-high house prices, a striking gap between what the wealthy and the rest pay — that make
the policies fit the country. It closes with a one-page map of the whole platform, so you know what
each of the other three courses covers.

A note on how this series reads: it **explains** TOP's policies; it does not campaign for or against
them. Where a number is the party's own claim, it says so ("TOP argues…"); where a fact comes from
Stats NZ or Inland Revenue, it says that too. And every policy is paired with its strongest critics —
because the point is to leave you able to argue *any* side, not to hand you a verdict.
