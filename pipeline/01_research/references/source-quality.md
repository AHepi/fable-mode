# Source Quality & Scoping (Stage 01 reference)

How to judge what goes into the research dossier.

## Trustworthy sources, in rough order

1. Standard textbooks and canonical treatments of the topic.
2. University course materials and lecture notes from reputable departments.
3. Peer-reviewed articles and well-established reference works (encyclopedias of mathematics, etc.).
4. Reputable expository writing (well-regarded explainers) — good for *framings and hooks*, checked
   against the above for facts.

Avoid as primary sources: anonymous forum answers, content farms, and anything you cannot
corroborate. If web access is unavailable, lean on well-established knowledge and **mark anything
uncertain** so the curriculum stage knows not to build on it.

## Scope to the assumed background

- Research the topic *as it would be taught to this audience*, not at full research depth.
- For each concept, note the minimum machinery needed to reach it from the assumed background.
- Prefer the simplest correct framing. A first course earns rigor gradually; do not import
  graduate-level generality a beginner cannot use.

## What to capture for each concept

- The idea in one plain sentence.
- What it depends on (its prerequisites).
- A concrete example or motivating problem.
- The misconception or sticking point learners hit, if there is a well-known one.

## Flagging

Mark every claim you are not confident in as **[verify]**. The curriculum and authoring stages treat
`[verify]` items as not-yet-trustworthy until checked.
