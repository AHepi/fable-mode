Abstract Algebra

Discover the hidden rules behind symmetry, arithmetic, and structure — a first course in group theory built entirely from high-school math.

Mathematics Foundational 11 modules ~5h Path · Pure Mathematics, From the Ground Up
Before you start
  • Comfortable manipulating algebraic expressions and solving equations
  • Familiarity with functions and basic geometry
  • No calculus or proof experience needed

Turn a square coaster a quarter turn and it looks untouched. Add five hours to nine o’clock and you land on two. Flip a triangle and spin it, or spin it and flip it, and you end up somewhere different each time. These look like three unrelated curiosities. They are the same idea in three disguises — and that idea, structure, is what this course is about.

Group theory is the mathematics of structure: of operations and the rules they obey, of symmetry made exact. You will build it from the ground up — clock arithmetic, the four rules that make a group, the symmetries of a triangle, subgroups and generators, and the one theorem (Lagrange’s) where counting alone forces the truth — and end at rings and fields, the doorway to the rest of algebra. Every idea is shown, not just stated: operation tables you can read, symmetries you can see, and one proof walked slowly enough to follow without ever having met a proof before. No calculus, no prior proof experience — just high-school algebra and a willingness to look closely.

Modules

  1. 01 The Secret Life of Symmetry What abstract algebra is really about — structure, not numbers — and why three everyday puzzles are secretly one idea. 12 min
  2. 02 Clock Arithmetic Adding on a clock face introduces a finite world of arithmetic that wraps around — our first concrete playground. 18 min
  3. 03 Operations and Their Rules Strip arithmetic down to its bones — a set, a rule for combining two elements, and four properties that turn out to matter more than the specific numbers involved. 20 min
  4. 04 What Is a Group? Bolt the four rules together and you get the single most-studied object in modern algebra — a group. 18 min
  5. 05 A Zoo of Groups Groups are everywhere once you know the shape — numbers, fractions, roots of unity, and more. 16 min
  6. 06 Symmetries of a Triangle The symmetries of an equilateral triangle form a group where order suddenly matters — the first time flip-then-spin and spin-then-flip part ways. 22 min
  7. 07 Subgroups Sometimes a group hides smaller groups inside it — and finding them reveals its structure. 18 min
  8. 08 Cyclic Groups and Generators Start with one element and keep applying it — sometimes you spin out the whole group. 18 min
  9. 09 Counting Tells the Truth — Lagrange's Theorem A subgroup's size cannot be just anything — it must divide the size of the whole group, and counting equal-sized slices shows why. 20 min
  10. 10 When Two Groups Are Secretly the Same Maps that respect the operation reveal when two different-looking groups are really the same group in disguise. 20 min
  11. 11 Beyond Groups: Rings and Fields Add a second operation and the landscape opens up — the integers, the rationals, and the structures that organize all of algebra. 18 min
