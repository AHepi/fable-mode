TOP 2026: The Essentials

The shared language for understanding The Opportunity Party's 2026 platform: the problem the party thinks New Zealand has, the handful of concepts its policies use (land value tax, a Citizen's Income, tax distortion), and the NZ facts every policy leans on.

Economics & Policy Beginner 3 modules ~0.8h Path · The Opportunity Party's 2026 Platform
Before you start
  • General awareness of NZ life — rates, rent, KiwiSaver, the cost of living
  • No economics or tax background needed

Every election, a small party turns up with a stack of policies and a pitch that sounds either visionary or mad, depending on who you ask. The Opportunity Party (TOP) — a minor New Zealand party that styles itself “radical centrism” — is one of those. Its 2026 platform is unusually systematic: a land tax, a guaranteed income, an energy plan, an ocean plan, a democracy fix. To argue about any one of them at the pub, you first need the few ideas they all share.

That is what this short primer gives you. It is the Essentials course of a four-part series, built for someone with no economics background who wants to understand these policies — well enough to discuss them, not just nod along. Across three modules it lays out TOP’s diagnosis of what’s gone wrong in New Zealand, the small toolkit of concepts its policies run on (what a land value tax is, what a Citizen’s Income is, why taxes “distort” behaviour), and the New Zealand facts — no capital gains tax, sky-high house prices, a striking gap between what the wealthy and the rest pay — that make the policies fit the country. It closes with a one-page map of the whole platform, so you know what each of the other three courses covers.

A note on how this series reads: it explains TOP’s policies; it does not campaign for or against them. Where a number is the party’s own claim, it says so (“TOP argues…”); where a fact comes from Stats NZ or Inland Revenue, it says that too. And every policy is paired with its strongest critics — because the point is to leave you able to argue any side, not to hand you a verdict.

Modules

  1. 01 The Diagnosis A beginner's story of why owning property has tended to make people better off faster than working — and why TOP thinks the rules, not people's choices, are to blame. 14 min
  2. 02 The Policy Toolkit The key words behind every TOP policy — defined one at a time in plain language, with everyday examples, so nothing in the rest of the series stops you cold. 16 min
  3. 03 The NZ Context, and a Map of the Platform The New Zealand numbers behind TOP's policies — in plain language — plus a friendly map of the whole 2026 platform so you know what each course covers. 15 min
