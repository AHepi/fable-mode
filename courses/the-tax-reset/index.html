The Tax Reset

The Opportunity Party's flagship package, taken apart: a Citizen's Income for almost every adult, a Land Value Tax to help pay for it, a simpler income tax — and an honest look at who gains, who pays, and what the critics say.

Economics & Policy Beginner 3 modules ~0.8h Path · The Opportunity Party's 2026 Platform
Before you start
  • Comfortable with percentages and everyday tax ideas (PAYE, GST, rates)
  • The Essentials course is the ideal warm-up, but this course stands on its own

If you only learn one of The Opportunity Party’s policies, learn this one. The Tax Reset is the party’s flagship — and its most radical bet: replace some of the tax New Zealand puts on working with a tax on owning land, and pay almost every adult a guaranteed Citizen’s Income. Pull the thread and the whole platform comes with it: how the land tax pays for the income, why the income tax gets simplified, why KiwiSaver becomes compulsory.

This short course takes the package apart, one piece at a time. The first module explains the Citizen’s Income — what it is, the welfare problems it targets, and why its modest size is the heart of the argument. The second explains the Land Value Tax that helps fund it, plus the income tax and KiwiSaver changes bundled alongside. The third is where it gets interesting: TOP’s claim that seventy per cent of households end up better off, who actually wins and loses, and the strongest objections from both the left and the right.

It stands on its own — where it leans on a shared idea (a land tax barely changes behaviour because “land can’t run away”; a Citizen’s Income is a floor everyone stands on), it reminds you in a line. As throughout the series, TOP’s own figures are attributed to TOP and the modelling is flagged as the party’s own; the goal is to leave you able to argue the package from any side.

Modules

  1. 01 A Citizen's Income What TOP's Citizen's Income actually is, the problem it targets, and why a poverty-level floor is the heart of the debate. 15 min
  2. 02 The Land Value Tax How TOP would tax the value of land — not buildings — to help fund the Citizen's Income, and why the package bundles a simpler income tax and compulsory KiwiSaver alongside it. 16 min
  3. 03 Winners, Losers and the Critiques TOP's "70/20/10" distributional claim, four concrete household stories, and the strongest objections from left and right — so you can argue any side of the Tax Reset. 15 min
