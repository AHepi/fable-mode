Democracy and Everyday Life

The Opportunity Party's plans for how New Zealand decides and lives: citizens' assemblies and cleaner politics to break gridlock, a Teal Card for under-30s, and changes to public transport and drug law — each with its rationale and its critics.

Economics & Policy Beginner 3 modules ~0.7h Path · The Opportunity Party's 2026 Platform
Before you start
  • General awareness of NZ politics and public services
  • The Essentials course is the ideal warm-up, but this course stands on its own

Not every policy is about money. Some are about how the country decides — and some are about the texture of ordinary life: getting to work, going to the dentist at twenty-four, what the law does about drugs. This short course gathers the rest of The Opportunity Party’s platform under one roof.

The first module covers Citizens’ Voice and cleaner politics — TOP’s plan to break “pendulum politics” with randomly chosen citizens’ assemblies, plus a lobbying register and donation limits. The second covers the Teal Card, a bundle of free healthcare, transport and a savings boost for under-30s, tied to a civic-service scheme. The third covers two everyday-life policies that often surprise people: permanent free public transport (paid for by road-user charges) and a harm-reduction approach to drugs, including cannabis legalisation.

These are contested policies, so the course is especially careful to stay even-handed: each is explained on its own terms and attributed to TOP, and each is paired with its strongest objections — the cost of free fares, the fairness of tying money to mandatory service, the public-safety arguments around drug reform and the 2020 referendum that narrowly rejected legalising cannabis. As ever, the aim is to leave you able to discuss them, not to tell you what to think.

Modules

  1. 01 Citizens' Voice and Cleaner Politics How TOP proposes to break gridlock on big issues — citizens' assemblies, a Parliamentary Commissioner, and a package of anti-corruption reforms — and what critics say about each. 15 min
  2. 02 The Teal Card TOP's proposed bundle of health, transport, and savings support for under-30s — its rationale, its components, and the genuine questions it raises. 14 min
  3. 03 Getting Around, and Drug Law Two everyday policies TOP proposes — permanent free public transport funded by road-user charges, and a harm-minimisation overhaul of drug law — each explained with its mechanism, the problem it targets, and the strongest objections. 14 min
