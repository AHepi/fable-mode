Homes, Energy and Oceans

The Opportunity Party's plan for a productive, green economy: how it would make housing affordable beyond the land tax, build abundant renewable energy, and protect a third of New Zealand's ocean while growing a blue economy — with the trade-offs in each.

Economics & Policy Beginner 3 modules ~0.7h Path · The Opportunity Party's 2026 Platform
Before you start
  • General awareness of NZ housing, power bills, and fishing/ocean issues
  • The Essentials course is the ideal warm-up, but this course stands on its own

The Opportunity Party calls itself “blue-green” — and three of its policies show what that means in practice: a plan for homes, a plan for energy, and a plan for the ocean, all argued as economic policy rather than charity or sentiment. Build enough houses and you fix affordability and free up capital; build abundant clean power and you run a modern economy cheaply; protect the sea and you grow the industries that depend on it.

This short course takes each in turn. The first module covers affordable housing — the zoning, consenting and building reforms TOP proposes beyond its land tax. The second covers abundant energy — a 25-year plan to triple renewable generation and treat power as the engine of growth. The third covers healthy oceans — protecting a third of New Zealand’s waters, ending bottom trawling, and growing a “blue economy,” with Treaty fishing rights in the frame.

Each module stands on its own and reminds you of any shared idea in a line. And as everywhere in this series, the policies are explained, not sold: TOP’s targets are attributed to TOP, and every plan is weighed against its real critics — the homeowners who fear falling prices, the cost of a 25-year energy build, the fishing communities a trawling ban would hit.

Modules

  1. 01 Affordable Housing Beyond the land tax — TOP's supply-side plan to untangle the rules that stop New Zealand building enough homes. 15 min
  2. 02 Abundant Energy A 25-year plan to triple New Zealand's renewable electricity — and what it would cost, who it would help, and why bipartisan ambition is easier promised than kept. 15 min
  3. 03 Healthy Oceans How TOP proposes to protect 30% of New Zealand's ocean, phase out bottom trawling, and grow a 'blue economy' — and what the fishing industry says in response. 14 min
