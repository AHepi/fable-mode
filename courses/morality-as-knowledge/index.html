Morality as Knowledge

The case, built on David Deutsch's and Karl Popper's epistemology, that morality is objective: moral knowledge has no foundation and needs none, because it grows the way all knowledge does, by conjecture, criticism, and error-correction.

Humanities Intermediate 10 modules ~5h
Before you start
  • Willingness to follow a sustained philosophical argument; no prior philosophy assumed

Ask people whether morality is objective and most will say no, it is just opinion, who is to say. Ask the same people whether the men who ran the death camps were merely doing something the speaker would have preferred they not do, or were wrong, and the slogan falls away. We say morality is subjective and we act as though some moral claims are simply correct. This course lives in that gap, and it argues that the slogan is the part that should give.

The case is built on the epistemology of David Deutsch and Karl Popper. Its pivot is a single move. People reject moral objectivity because morality has no foundation: you cannot prove that slavery is wrong from some bedrock of certainty, and you cannot derive an “ought” from an “is.” All true. But nothing rests on a foundation of that kind, not even physics, where no number of observations ever proves a theory. Knowledge is never derived from a base; it is guessed, criticized, and corrected. So the lack of a foundation is the universal condition of knowledge, not a special defect of morality, and “objective” never meant “proven.” It means correctable: answerable to more than opinion, able to be got wrong and put right. By that standard, which is the only standard any knowledge ever met, moral knowledge can be objective, and the reality of moral progress (the abolition of slavery was the correction of an error, not a change of fashion) is the sign that it is.

You will meet the strongest objections first and in their own terms, from Hume, Moore, Mackie, and Ayer, before any reply touches them, and you will see exactly which thinker said what, Popper kept distinct from Deutsch. The course argues hard for its thesis and is equally clear about where the thesis stops: it shows morality stands on the same footing as science and that moral progress is real, but whether that secures full moral realism is a question serious people still dispute, and the final module leaves it honestly open. What you take away is not a proof. It is a way of holding any morality, including your own, as a conjecture open to criticism.

Modules

  1. 01 What 'Objective' Would Even Mean Almost no one really believes the Holocaust was wrong only as a matter of taste, yet almost everyone says morality is subjective, and the gap between what we say and what we do is where this course lives. 26 min
  2. 02 Morality Looks Like Opinion The case that morality is just feeling or culture is stronger than its slogans, and it has to be met at full strength before it can be answered. 28 min
  3. 03 The Case Against Moral Facts Three sharp philosophical arguments, often blurred into one, say there are no moral facts to be objective about, and they are the real obstacle the course must clear. 30 min
  4. 04 Knowledge Without Foundations Popper's answer to a problem older than ethics: no knowledge rests on a foundation, not even science, and yet knowledge grows by conjecture and criticism. 30 min
  5. 05 No Special Problem The objection that you cannot get an ought from an is turns out not to be special to morality, because you cannot get a scientific theory from observations either, and that changes everything. 28 min
  6. 06 Good Explanations, Hard to Vary If proof is not what makes knowledge objective, something else must be, and Deutsch's answer is the explanation that is hard to vary. 28 min
  7. 07 Moral Progress Is Real The abolition of slavery was not a change of fashion but the correction of an error, and that single fact is very hard for relativism to survive. 28 min
  8. 08 Knowledge and the Good Popper built the political form of error-correction, a society that can remove its rulers without bloodshed, and Deutsch ties the good itself to the growth of knowledge and the evil to its entrenchment. 28 min
  9. 09 Is Beauty Objective Too? If beauty, the one thing nearly everyone is certain is mere taste, turns out to be objective in a domain as cold as the coevolution of flowers and insects, then objectivity without a foundation is a wider thing than anyone supposed. 26 min
  10. 10 What This Settles, and What It Doesn't The honest accounting: the case has shown morality is on the same footing as science and that moral progress is real, but whether that secures full moral realism is a question serious people still dispute. 28 min
